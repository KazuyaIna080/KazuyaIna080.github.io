Figure 3 Drift-Phase vs Decision-Phase Wars / 漂流相 vs 決定相の戦争
● Drift-Phase War / 漂流相
  • Passage: Gradual, months/years
  • Awareness: "We can still turn back"
  • Conditions: Sequential emergence
  • Victory belief: Cannot win, but no alternative
  • Example: Japan 1941, WWI 1914
● Decision-Phase War / 決定相
  • Passage: Instantaneous, hours/days
  • Awareness: "We must act now"
  • Conditions: Simultaneous emergence
  • Victory belief: Will win if we fight
  • Example: America 1941 (post-Pearl Harbor)

Two fundamentally different paths to war, requiring different prevention strategies
